Timeline of the French Revolution

Last edited: 2025-11-08 02:40 UTC

First version: 2025-11-08 02:40 UTC

Broad timeline of the events of the French Revolution. This page is a good starting point to get the general gist of events before diving into the details.

5 May 1789
Formation of the Estates-General. This is the first Estates-General in 175 years. The assembly is called to discuss the financial, economic, and social problems facing France. The Estates are the clergy (the First Estate), the nobility (the Second Estate), and the commoners, working-class and bourgeoisie (the Third Estate).
17 June 1789 — 9 July 1789
Due to dissatisfaction over the structure and decisions of the Estates-General, the Third Estate leaves to form their own body called the National Assembly. Soon, some members of the First and Second Estate join them. Political pressure eventually forces Louis XVI to recognize its legitimacy.
9 July 1789 — 30 September 1791
National Assembly transforms into the National Constituent Assembly for the purpose of drafting a new constitution. Assembly grows popular with Parisians and the increased presence of Royal troops brings fears that the military will be used to forcibly disperse it. This leads to the Storming of the Bastille.
14 July 1789
Storming of the Bastille.
June 1791
King Louis XVI attempts to flee to Montmédy, but is captured along the way.
April 1792
French Revolutionary Wars break out, with other European states attempting to prevent the spread of revolution or take advantage of French weakness.
10 August 1792
Insurrection against the French monarchy. Armed revolutionaries in Paris storm Tuileries Palace.
21-22 September 1792
Formal end of the monarchy and establishment of a republic.
21 January 1793
Execution of King Louis the XVI.
June 1793
Third revolt/insurrection. Suspension of the constitution. Power passes to Committee of Public Safety, dominated by Jacobins and Robespierre, starting the Reign of Terror.
16 October 1793
Execution of Marie Antoinette.
November 1795
Replacement of the Committee of Public Safety by the Directory.
9 November 1799
Coup of 18 Brumaire. Establishment of the Consulate with Napoleon as First Consul.
